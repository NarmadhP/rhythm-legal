Rhythm App - Data Deletion Instructions

üìå About Your Data in Rhythm

Rhythm is a privacy-first app that uses anonymous authentication and stores all your health data locally on your device. We do not have access to your medication information or personal details.

Since there are no traditional "accounts" with email/password, data deletion is straightforward and happens directly on your device.

What Data Does Rhythm Store?

1. Local Device Data (Stored on Your Phone/Tablet)

  • Medication Database: All your medications, schedules, and history (stored in encrypted SQLite database)
  • Anonymous User ID: A random UUID used only for subscription management (stored in iOS Keychain or Android Encrypted Shared Preferences)
  • App Preferences: Settings like notifications, themes, etc.

2. Optional Cloud Sync Data (Only if You Enabled Cloud Sync)

  • iOS: A copy of your medication database in your private iCloud Drive container (iCloud.com.rhythm.medapp)
  • Android: A copy of your medication database in your private Google Drive AppData folder (hidden from Google Drive UI)

Note: Cloud sync is disabled by default and requires a Premium subscription. Only you can access this data.

3. Subscription Data (Managed by RevenueCat)

  • Anonymous User ID: Your UUID linked to subscription status
  • Purchase History: Transaction records for billing purposes
  • Platform: iOS or Android

Note: RevenueCat does not store your medication or health data, only subscription information.

4. Crash Reports (Firebase Crashlytics)

  • Anonymous Crash Logs: Device model, OS version, app version, crash stack traces

Note: No personal or health data is included in crash reports. Data auto-deletes after 90 days.

How to Delete Your Data

1Delete Local Data (Medication Database)

This is the most important step as it contains all your medication information.

Method 1: Delete All Data in App (Recommended)

  1. Open the Rhythm app
  2. Go to Settings
  3. Scroll to Advanced section
  4. Tap "Delete All Data"
  5. Confirm deletion

‚úÖ This will permanently delete all medications, schedules, and history from your device.

Method 2: Uninstall the App

  • iOS: Long-press the Rhythm app icon ‚Üí Tap "Remove App" ‚Üí "Delete App"
  • Android: Long-press the Rhythm app icon ‚Üí Tap "Uninstall"

‚úÖ Uninstalling the app will delete all local data, including your anonymous user ID.

2Delete Cloud Sync Data (If You Enabled Cloud Sync)

Skip this step if you never enabled cloud sync or never subscribed to Premium.

For iOS (iCloud Drive):

  1. Open the Rhythm app
  2. Go to Settings ‚Üí Cloud Sync
  3. Toggle "Cloud Sync" OFF
  4. The app will delete the database from iCloud Drive

Alternative: Delete manually from iCloud Drive:

  1. Open the Files app on your iPhone/iPad
  2. Tap "iCloud Drive"
  3. Find the folder "Rhythm" or "iCloud.com.rhythm.medapp"
  4. Swipe left ‚Üí Tap "Delete"

For Android (Google Drive AppData):

  1. Open the Rhythm app
  2. Go to Settings ‚Üí Cloud Sync
  3. Toggle "Cloud Sync" OFF
  4. The app will delete the database from Google Drive AppData

Note: Google Drive AppData is hidden and cannot be accessed manually. Disabling cloud sync or uninstalling the app will automatically delete it.

3Cancel Subscription (If You Have Premium)

Cancelling your subscription prevents future charges but does not delete subscription history.

For iOS:

  1. Open the Settings app on your iPhone/iPad
  2. Tap your name at the top ‚Üí Subscriptions
  3. Find "Rhythm Premium"
  4. Tap "Cancel Subscription"

For Android:

  1. Open the Google Play Store app
  2. Tap your profile icon (top right) ‚Üí Payments & subscriptions ‚Üí Subscriptions
  3. Find "Rhythm Premium"
  4. Tap "Cancel subscription"

4Request Deletion of Subscription Data (Optional)

If you want to delete your subscription history from RevenueCat, please contact us with your:

  • Anonymous User ID (found in Settings ‚Üí Advanced ‚Üí User ID)
  • Platform: iOS or Android
  • Reason for deletion: (optional)

We will forward your request to RevenueCat to delete your subscription data within 30 days.
