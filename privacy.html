Privacy Policy

Last Updated:

Narmadh P ("we," "our," or "us") operates the Rhythm mobile application ("the App" or "the Service"). This Privacy Policy informs you of our policies regarding the collection, use, and disclosure of personal data when you use our Service and the choices you have associated with that data.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Health and Medication Data

Local Storage First

All health and medication data you enter into the Rhythm app is stored primarily on your device in a local encrypted database. This includes:

  • Medication names and dosages
  • Medication schedules and reminders
  • Medication history and logs
  • Adherence statistics and analytics
  • Any other health-related information you input

We do not collect, transmit, or store this sensitive health information on our servers. This data remains under your control and is accessible only through your device.

1.2 Anonymous User Identification

The Rhythm app does not require email addresses, passwords, or personal account creation. Instead, we use a completely anonymous authentication system:

  • A unique anonymous identifier (UUID) is generated and stored securely on your device
  • On iOS: This identifier is stored in the device's Keychain with iCloud Keychain sync enabled (if you have iCloud enabled)
  • On Android: This identifier is stored in Encrypted Shared Preferences with automatic Google Drive Backup (if you have Android backup enabled)
  • This identifier is used solely for subscription management via RevenueCat
  • Optional recovery secret (user-created phrase) allows you to restore your subscription on new devices

We cannot identify you personally from this anonymous identifier, and we do not collect or store your name, email, phone number, or any personally identifiable information.

1.3 Optional Cloud Sync (Premium Feature)

If you subscribe to Premium and enable cloud sync, your medication data is synced to your personal cloud storage:

  • iOS users: Data is synced to your private iCloud Drive container (iCloud.com.rhythm.medapp) using iCloud Documents. This data is accessible only to your Apple ID and is encrypted by Apple.
  • Android users: Data is synced to your private Google Drive AppData folder (hidden from Google Drive UI) using the drive.appdata scope. This data is accessible only by this app and is deleted when you uninstall the app.

Cloud sync is entirely optional, disabled by default, and requires Premium subscription. You retain full control and can disable cloud sync at any time. When disabled, all data remains local on your device only.

1.4 Crash Reports and Error Logs

We use Firebase Crashlytics to collect anonymous crash reports and error logs to help us improve app stability. This information may include:

  • Device information (device model, operating system version)
  • App version information
  • Crash stack traces and error messages
  • Anonymous crash identifiers

Important: Firebase Crashlytics does not collect your personal health data, medication information, or personally identifiable information. All crash data is anonymized and used solely for debugging purposes.

1.5 Subscription and Purchase Information

If you subscribe to Rhythm Premium, RevenueCat processes your purchase information using your anonymous identifier. This may include:

  • Your anonymous user ID (UUID)
  • Purchase history and transaction identifiers
  • Subscription status (active, expired, cancelled)
  • Platform (iOS or Android)

We do not have access to your payment card information or personal billing details, which are handled securely by the app store (Apple App Store or Google Play Store). RevenueCat only shares subscription status information with our app to enable premium features.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the minimal information we collect to:

  • Provide, maintain, and improve the Service
  • Manage your anonymous user identifier for subscription purposes
  • Process your subscription status and enable premium features
  • Sync your medication data to your personal cloud storage (if you enable cloud sync)
  • Diagnose and fix crashes and technical issues
  • Respond to your inquiries and provide customer support
  • Detect and prevent fraud or abuse of subscriptions

We do not:

  • Sell or share your data with third parties for marketing purposes
  • Use your health or medication data for any purpose other than providing the Service to you
  • Track your identity across other apps or websites
  • Send promotional emails or push notifications (we have no email address to send to)

3. Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services that may collect or process information:

3.1 Firebase Crashlytics

Firebase Crashlytics is used to collect anonymous crash reports and error logs to help us improve app stability. Google's Firebase Privacy Policy applies to this service. You can review it at: https://firebase.google.com/support/privacy

3.2 RevenueCat

RevenueCat manages subscriptions and in-app purchases using your anonymous user identifier. Their Privacy Policy can be found at: https://www.revenuecat.com/privacy

RevenueCat processes your subscription status and validates purchases with Apple App Store and Google Play Store on your behalf.

3.3 Apple iCloud Drive (iOS Only, Optional)

If you enable cloud sync on iOS, your medication database is stored in your private iCloud Drive container. Apple's iCloud Privacy Policy applies: https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/

This data is only accessible to your Apple ID and is encrypted by Apple. We do not have access to your iCloud data.

3.4 Google Drive AppData (Android Only, Optional)

If you enable cloud sync on Android, your medication database is stored in your private Google Drive AppData folder using the restricted drive.appdata scope. Google's Privacy Policy applies: https://policies.google.com/privacy

This data is hidden from the Google Drive UI and is only accessible by this app. The data is deleted when you uninstall the app. We do not have access to your Google Drive data.

3.5 Apple App Store & Google Play Store

When you make a purchase, payment processing is handled entirely by Apple App Store or Google Play Store according to their respective privacy policies. We never see or store your payment card information.

4. Data Security

We take data security seriously and implement multiple layers of protection:

  • Local Database: Your health and medication data is stored in an encrypted SQLite database on your device using the Drift/Moor library with secure storage.
  • Anonymous Identifier: Your anonymous user ID is stored in secure storage:
    • iOS: Stored in iOS Keychain with iCloud Keychain sync (if enabled)
    • Android: Stored in Encrypted Shared Preferences with backup encryption (if enabled)
  • Cloud Sync Encryption: When enabled, cloud sync uses platform-native encryption:
    • iOS: iCloud Drive encryption managed by Apple
    • Android: Google Drive encryption managed by Google
  • No Server Storage: We do not operate any servers that store your personal or health data. All data remains on your device or in your personal cloud storage.
  • Transmission Security: All communications with RevenueCat and Firebase are encrypted using SSL/TLS.

However, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security. Your device's security (passcode, biometrics) is the primary protection for your local data.

5. Data Retention

Health and Medication Data: This data remains on your device until you delete it or uninstall the App. If you enable cloud sync, a copy also remains in your personal iCloud Drive or Google Drive until you disable cloud sync or delete the data.

Anonymous User ID: Your anonymous identifier remains in secure storage until you uninstall the App or manually delete it. If you have iCloud Keychain sync (iOS) or Android backup enabled, it may persist in your cloud backup.

Subscription Data: RevenueCat retains your anonymous subscription history according to their data retention policies and as required for financial compliance.

Crash Reports: Firebase Crashlytics retains anonymized crash data according to Google's data retention policies (typically 90 days).

6. Your Rights and Choices

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

6.1 Access and Control

  • Health Data: You can access, modify, or delete your health and medication data directly within the App at any time. This data is entirely under your control.
  • Anonymous User ID: Your anonymous identifier is automatically generated and cannot be changed, but you can generate a new one by uninstalling and reinstalling the App. Note that this will require re-subscribing to Premium if applicable.
  • Cloud Sync: You can enable or disable cloud sync at any time through the App settings. When disabled, your data remains local only.
  • Recovery Secret: You can optionally create a recovery secret phrase in Settings → Advanced → Recovery Secret to restore your subscription on new devices.
  • Data Deletion: You can delete all your medication data within the App. Uninstalling the App will delete all local data and your anonymous identifier. Cloud sync data (if enabled) will be removed from iCloud Drive or Google Drive AppData when you disable cloud sync or uninstall the app.

6.2 Data Portability

Since your health data is stored locally on your device and in your personal cloud storage (if enabled), you have full control over it:

  • Export: You can export your medication data as CSV or PDF directly from the App (Settings → Export Data)
  • Backup: Your device's backup system (iCloud Backup on iOS, Google Backup on Android) may include your medication database
  • Cloud Sync: When enabled, you can access your medication database file in your iCloud Drive or Google Drive (though it's in SQLite format)

6.3 Opt-Out Options

  • You can disable crash reporting by turning off Firebase Crashlytics data collection (contact us for instructions)
  • You can disable push notifications through your device settings
  • You can disable cloud sync through the App settings (Settings → Cloud Sync)
  • You can cancel subscriptions through your app store account settings

6.4 California Privacy Rights

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), including the right to know what personal information is collected, the right to delete personal information, and the right to opt-out of the sale of personal information. Note that we do not sell your personal information.

6.5 European Privacy Rights

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have certain data protection rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), including:

  • The right to access, update, or delete your personal information
  • The right to rectification
  • The right to object
  • The right to restriction of processing
  • The right to data portability
  • The right to withdraw consent

7. Children's Privacy

The Service is not intended for children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personally identifiable information from children under 13. Since the App uses anonymous authentication and stores all data locally on the device, there is minimal personal information to collect. However, if you are a parent or guardian and believe your child is using the App, please be aware that they can manage medications and set reminders. If you have concerns, please contact us. The App requires no registration or personal information, making it difficult for us to identify or remove specific user data, but you can delete all data by uninstalling the App.

10. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a data breach that affects your information, we will notify you and relevant authorities as required by applicable law. Given that we do not operate servers storing your personal or health data, and all such data is stored locally on your device or in your personal cloud storage, the risk of data breaches is significantly minimized. Any potential breach would likely involve third-party services (RevenueCat, Firebase Crashlytics, iCloud, or Google Drive), and we would be notified by those providers according to their breach notification procedures.
